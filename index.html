About Me

Hello! I'm Scott Faust, and I'm on a journey to harness the transformative power of technology as a software engineer. My passion lies in not just understanding the intricacies of code but in using it as a tool to make a real-world impact.

Born and raised in the nurturing environment of Denver, Colorado, my fascination with technology started at a young age. I found myself intrigued by the endless possibilities that software engineering presented – it was like having a language to converse with the future. Joining the Airforce right out of high school, I served as an Aircraft Mechanic, where I got my first hands-on experience with sophisticated technology. This experience wasn't just about understanding machinery; it was a lesson in precision, discipline, and the importance of every small part in a larger system.

After my retirement in 2018, I embarked on a new mission – to become a software engineer. My journey at the University of Nebraska at Omaha isn't just an academic pursuit; it's a path to realizing my dream of making the world a better place. Here, I'm not only honing my skills in coding and software development but also learning how to leverage these skills to create accessible, user-friendly solutions.

My vision is a world where technology is not just an elite privilege but a tool accessible to everyone, everywhere. I believe that in the right hands, technology can be a great equalizer, breaking down barriers and opening doors to limitless possibilities. Whether it's developing applications that improve everyday life, creating systems that make information more accessible, or contributing to groundbreaking innovations, I'm committed to using my skills for the greater good.

As I stride forward in my career, I carry with me a commitment to excellence, a passion for learning, and a steadfast belief that technology, when used wisely and ethically, can indeed make the world a better place for all.

Education

University of Nebraska at Omaha (UNO)

Bachelor of Science in Computer Science, Focus on Software Engineering
2020 - Present

Embarking on a journey to redefine my career path, I joined the University of Nebraska at Omaha to pursue a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science, with a special focus on Software Engineering. This decision marked a pivotal point in my life - a transition from a decorated career in the Airforce as an Aircraft Mechanic to delving into the intricacies of software development and engineering.

At UNO, my academic experience has been a blend of rigorous coursework, practical projects, and collaborative learning. I've immersed myself in a variety of subjects such as algorithms, system design, database management, and software development practices. Each class has not only contributed to a solid foundation in computer science principles but has also sparked creativity and innovation in solving real-world problems.

Metropolitan Community College

Associate's Degree in Applied Science and Information Technology, Computer Programming
2018 - 2020

Prior to UNO, my academic pursuits led me to Metropolitan Community College, where I earned my Associate's Degree in Applied Science and Information Technology with a specialization in Computer Programming. It was here that my passion for technology found its first formal expression. The curriculum provided me with a comprehensive understanding of programming fundamentals, web development, and the basics of computer systems.

This program was instrumental in shaping my initial understanding of the tech world. It laid the groundwork for my future studies and offered a glimpse into the potential of software engineering to drive change and innovation.

Continuous Learning

Driven by an insatiable thirst for knowledge, my education extends beyond formal institutions. I am constantly exploring new technologies, keeping up-to-date with industry trends, and engaging in online courses and communities. This ongoing commitment to learning ensures that I stay relevant and versatile in a field that is ever-evolving.
